Moving a studio is not like moving a home. Clay is heavy, glazes are chemistry, and a kiln does not enjoy travelling. When I left Saint Petersburg for a small town near Nuremberg in 2023, I had to decide what was truly mine to carry.

Most of the tools came. My favourite trimming loop, worn smooth by years, made the trip in my coat pocket. The glaze recipes came as a notebook, though the German minerals behave just differently enough that every recipe needed re-testing from scratch.

What surprised me was how much stayed the same. The wheel spins the same way in any country. The clay still remembers the pressure of your hands. Rebuilding the shelves was slow, but it taught me which pieces of my old practice were habits, and which were truly me.
