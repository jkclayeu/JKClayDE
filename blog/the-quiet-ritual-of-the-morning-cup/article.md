There is a difference between drinking coffee and having a cup of coffee. The first is fuel; the second is a small ceremony. The vessel is what turns one into the other.

A handmade cup asks to be held. The weight sits differently in the palm, the rim meets your lip with a softness a machine-made edge never has, and the faint unevenness of the glaze catches the morning light. You notice it — and noticing is the whole point.

When you choose a cup, choose the one that makes you pause. Pick it up. Feel where your fingers fall. The right one will quietly ask you to sit down for two more minutes, and those two minutes will change the shape of your morning.
