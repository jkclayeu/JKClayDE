Handmade stoneware is tougher than it looks. My pieces are fired hot and glazed to be food-safe, dishwasher-safe and microwave-safe. You do not need to treat them as museum objects — they are made to be used every day.

That said, a few small kindnesses help. A gentle hand-wash keeps a glaze glowing longer than repeated dishwasher cycles. Avoid sudden temperature shocks — do not move a piece straight from the freezer to a hot oven — as thermal stress is the one thing that can crack good stoneware.

Over the years a well-used piece develops a soft patina, a record of the meals and mornings it has shared. That is not wear to be avoided; it is the piece becoming yours.
